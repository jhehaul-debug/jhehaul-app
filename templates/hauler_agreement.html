Independent Contractor, Marketplace Participation & Non-Circumvention Agreement

Company: JHE HAUL, operated by J Hunter Enterprises Inc. ("Company")
Platform: JHE HAUL mobile and web application ("Platform")
User: Independent junk removal service provider ("Hauler")
Effective Date: Upon account registration

1. Legal Acceptance

By creating an account, logging in, bidding on a job, or accepting a job through the Platform, the Hauler agrees to this Agreement.

These actions constitute a legally binding electronic signature under the Electronic Signatures in Global and National Commerce Act (15 U.S.C. §7001).

If the Hauler does not agree, they must not use the Platform.

2. Age Eligibility & Qualification Requirements

You must be at least 18 years old to register as a Hauler or use the Platform in any capacity.

By creating a Hauler account, the Hauler represents and warrants that:

  • They are at least 18 years of age
  • They possess a valid driver's license as required by law for the operation of their vehicle(s)
  • Their vehicle(s) carry current and valid registration as required by applicable law
  • They maintain insurance coverage that is legally required for the operation of their vehicle(s) and for the hauling/removal services they perform
  • They are legally authorized to perform hauling, moving, and junk removal services in their service area
  • They have reviewed and comply with all local, state, and federal laws governing the hauling and disposal of materials in the jurisdictions where they operate

Disclaimer — No Verification by JHE HAUL: JHE HAUL does not independently verify, certify, or guarantee any Hauler's driver's license, vehicle registration, insurance coverage, legal compliance, or authorization to perform services. Haulers are responsible for maintaining all required credentials. Customers use Hauler services at their own discretion.

Accounts created by individuals under 18 are prohibited and will be terminated upon discovery.

3. Independent Contractor Status

The Hauler is an independent contractor and not an employee, agent, partner, or representative of the Company.

The Company:

  • Does not hire the Hauler
  • Does not supervise work
  • Does not control job performance
  • Does not provide equipment or labor
  • Does not guarantee jobs

The Hauler alone is responsible for:

  • Licenses
  • Insurance
  • Taxes
  • Workers
  • Vehicles
  • Disposal compliance
  • All applicable laws

4. Hauler Operations, Safety & Compliance

The Hauler assumes full and sole responsibility for all aspects of their operations, including but not limited to:

  • Vehicle safety and maintenance — ensuring vehicles are roadworthy and compliant with applicable safety standards
  • Operational safety — protecting themselves, their workers, customers, and the public during all service activities
  • Insurance — maintaining adequate auto, liability, and any other insurance coverage required by law or reasonably necessary for their services
  • Legal compliance — adhering to all applicable local, state, and federal laws, including waste disposal, environmental, labor, and transportation regulations
  • Licensing and permits — obtaining and maintaining any business licenses, hauling permits, or professional certifications required in their jurisdiction
  • Worker safety — providing a safe working environment for any employees, helpers, or subcontractors they engage

JHE HAUL is not responsible for any incidents, injuries, accidents, property damage, fines, violations, or legal liability arising from a Hauler's operations.

5. Platform Role

JHE HAUL is a technology marketplace only that connects customers with independent haulers.

The Company is NOT:

  • a junk removal company
  • a moving company
  • a contractor
  • a subcontractor
  • a carrier

The service contract exists solely between the Customer and the Hauler.

6. In-App Communication Requirement

All communication must occur inside the Platform.

The Hauler shall not:

  • Exchange phone numbers
  • Exchange emails
  • Leave business cards
  • Provide outside contact information
  • Direct the customer off the app

7. Non-Circumvention

The Hauler may not bypass the Platform.

The Hauler shall not perform services outside the Platform for any customer introduced through JHE HAUL.

This applies:

  • During use of the Platform
  • For two (2) years after leaving the Platform

Includes:

  • Repeat customers
  • Property managers
  • Referrals
  • Associated persons

Cancelling a job in the app and completing it privately is circumvention.

8. Liquidated Damages

Violation of Section 7 results in:

$2,500 per violation OR three (3) times the job value, whichever is greater.

The Company may also:

  • Remove the Hauler
  • Restrict access
  • Seek legal enforcement

9. Booking Fee, Bid Acceptance & Customer Payment

Hauler Bid

The Hauler submits a price ("Bid") for a job through the Platform. The Bid represents the total service price the Hauler is willing to perform the job for.

The Company does not set or control the Hauler's price.

Customer Booking Fee (Platform Fee)

When a customer accepts a Hauler's Bid, the customer must pay a required booking/platform fee to the Company in order to reserve the job.

The booking fee:

  • Is paid directly to JHE HAUL
  • Is not payment for junk removal services
  • Is a technology access, scheduling, and lead-generation fee
  • Is deducted from the Hauler's total Bid price

The Hauler has no control over the platform fee amount.

The booking fee is generally non-refundable except if:

  • The Hauler cancels the job, OR
  • The Company cannot provide a hauler for the scheduled service

Remaining Balance Paid to Hauler

After the job is completed, the customer pays the remaining balance of the Hauler's Bid directly to the Hauler.

The Company:

  • Does not collect the hauling payment
  • Does not hold hauling funds
  • Does not distribute hauling funds
  • Is not an escrow agent

The Hauler is solely responsible for:

  • Collecting the remaining payment
  • Refunds
  • Disputes
  • Receipts
  • Taxes

No Payment Obligation Owed by Hauler to Company

The Hauler does not pay the platform fee and never owes payment to the Company for a completed job.

The platform fee is paid solely by the customer as a booking and lead reservation fee associated with the Hauler's accepted Bid.
